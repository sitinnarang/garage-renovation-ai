Garage Slatwall FAQ

Common questions about our slatwall panel systems

Slatwall panels are durable, interlocking panels installed on garage walls to create efficient vertical storage systems. They feature horizontal grooves that accommodate various storage accessories like hooks, shelves, baskets, and bins. These accessories can be easily repositioned to adapt to changing storage needs, allowing you to organize tools, sports gear, cleaning supplies, and more while maximizing floor space.

Our slatwall panels are made from industrial-grade rigid cellular PVC (polyvinyl chloride). This material is superior to MDF or particle board alternatives because it's waterproof, won't rot or warp, and is resistant to moisture common in garage environments. The cellular foam construction is lightweight yet incredibly strong.

Yes! Our slatwall panels can be installed onto bare studs, unfinished drywall, finished drywall, and even concrete block walls. Our professional installers will assess your walls and use the appropriate mounting method to ensure a secure installation that can handle the full weight capacity.

Our slatwall panels have a load capacity of 40 lbs per square inch and 300 lbs per panel when properly installed. For heavy items like bicycles or large power tools, we recommend distributing weight across multiple hooks or using reinforced brackets. Our design consultants can help plan your layout for optimal weight distribution.

Slatwall panels are very easy to maintain. Simply wipe them down with a damp cloth and mild household cleaner as needed. The PVC construction is resistant to most chemicals, oils, and stains commonly found in garages. Avoid using abrasive cleaners that could scratch the surface finish.

Most slatwall installations can be completed in 1-2 days depending on the size of your garage and complexity of the installation. Our installers handle all cutting, fitting around obstacles, and finishing work including color-matched trim and screws for a professional appearance.
